Tare Zameen Foundation wishes you and your family well in these tough times.

By way of introduction, The Tare Zameen Foundation has been working for a better future for children, women and divyangjan. The foundation has grown immensely since its inception in 2018, and how! A small group of passionate individuals began their journey to improve children's access to education, provide employment opportunities to women and conduct various awareness campaigns for the benefit of the uneducated.

Our founder, Mr. Bishikesan Saw believes in collaborative efforts that can change the life of a community. He says, our efforts might not change the world immediately, but they will transform the life of needy individuals for the better. Our team strives for providing easy and accessible education for young children and improving women's access to healthcare.

The organisation has been staying true to Mr. Saw's vision and belief. We organized multiple campaigns where children were the main benefactory. The Meri Pehchan project and drawing competitions provide underprivileged children with a platform to improve their access to education and work on their mental well-being.  We are proud to admit that our efforts in the past year have touched the lives of over 600 kids so far.

We are also relentlessly working for issues pertaining to women health, hygiene and girl child literacy.  Our undertakings such as the Sakha project, community awareness programs, and domestic abuse awareness seminars have helped over 2300 women and girls. Our on-ground teams have distributed sanitary napkins and hygiene kits, alongside having active conversations that dispel menstruation myths.

The year 2020 presented us with novel problems that seemed never ending. While the world was in the grip of a pandemic, the poor and underprivileged had lost their source of livelihood and money. To ease financial and mental pressures during this time, Tare Zameen Foundation donated relief kits containing daily essentials like atta, pulses, rice, oil etc. Tea distribution campaigns were held and relief kits were distributed to all frontline workers to appreciate them for their efforts. During winters, we again went out to alleviate hardships of the needy and provide some much-needed warmth. A blanket distribution campaign was held, where 70+ individuals were given relief from the harsh and chilly winters. 

Our tireless efforts and 50+ campaigns have impacted the lives of over 3600 people. However, we were not alone. Our patrons and donors have always kept faith in us and supported us throughout the journey.
Our team at TZF would be grateful if you could contribute in crafting a better future for the underprivileged and needy.  Join hands with TZF by donating for our campaigns and partake in making a brighter future for women, children and divyangjan.
